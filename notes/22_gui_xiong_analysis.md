## The Gui-Xiong construction (arXiv:2205.05420, J. Algebra 657, 2024)

For a vector space `V` of dimension `n` with `G`-action (any group), set
- `Λ = Λ(V)`: exterior algebra of `V`.
- `Λ* = Λ(V*)`: exterior algebra of dual.
- `H'_{n, m} := ⊕_{i=0}^m (Λ^i ⊗ Λ*^{m-i})`: bigraded module at "internal level" m.

External degree on summand `Λ^i ⊗ Λ*^{m-i}` is `-m + 2i`.

**Their explicit Lefschetz operator:**
> `L := Σ_{k=1}^n e_{θ_k} ⊗ i_{θ_k}` in End(H'_{n, m})

where `{θ_k}` is a basis for `V`, `e_θ` is exterior product (multiplication by θ in Λ), `i_θ` is interior product (contraction by θ in Λ*).

**Their main theorem (Thm 12).** On `H'_{n,m}`, the Kähler package holds equivariantly:
- (PD) S_n-equivariant non-degenerate Poincaré pairing.
- (HL) Hard Lefschetz: `L^i: H'^{-i} → H'^i` is isomorphism for all `i ≥ 0`.
- (HR) Hodge-Riemann bilinear relations on primitive subspaces.

And Remark 9: this extends to any representation `V` of any group `G`.

## Direct correspondence with our setup

This is *exactly* our setup, with one twist:

| Our notation | Gui-Xiong notation |
|---|---|
| `R(M)` (= matroid face ring, exterior) | `Λ(V)` (free exterior) |
| `R^∨(M)` (matroid dual) | `Λ(V*)` (free dual) |
| `S(M) = R(M) ⊗ R^∨(M)` | `H'_{n,m}` (specific bigrade `m`) |
| `L = Σ x_i ⊗ x_i` | `L = Σ e_{θ_k} ⊗ i_{θ_k}` (identical) |
| Aut(M)-equivariance | G-equivariance for any G |

**Our L is *literally* Gui-Xiong's L**, but acting on the matroid quotient `R(M) ⊗ R^∨(M)` rather than the free `Λ ⊗ Λ*`.

## The key calculation: does Gui-Xiong's HL descend to the matroid quotient?

`R(M) = Λ(V) / I_M` where `I_M` is the ideal generated by circuit relations.

`L` preserves the ideal `I_M` (computed directly: `L(x_C ⊗ y_T) = Σ_{i ∉ C, i ∈ T} x_{C ∪ i} ⊗ y_{T \ i}`, and `x_{C ∪ i}` is also a circuit-superset, hence in `I_M`).

So `L` descends to `L_quot` on `R(M) ⊗ R^∨(M)`. The question is whether `L_quot` is injective at our bigrades.

**Key observation:** Under projection `π: Λ ⊗ Λ* → R ⊗ R^∨`:
- `ker(L_quot) = π(π^{-1}(ker L_quot)) = π({ṽ ∈ Λ ⊗ Λ* : L_amb(ṽ) ∈ I_M target})`.
- By Gui-Xiong's HL, `L_amb` is *injective* at our bigrade (= ascending direction below middle), so `(L_amb)^{-1}` is well-defined.
- `ker(L_quot) = 0` ⟺ `(L_amb)^{-1}(I_M target) ⊆ I_M source` ⟺ `L_amb(I_M source) ⊇ I_M target`.

Combined with `L_amb(I_M source) ⊆ I_M target` (preservation), we get:

> `L_quot` is injective at bigrade `(m, -d) → (m+1, -d+2)` if and only if `L_amb(I_M source) = I_M target` exactly.

This is a dimension equality:
`dim I_M^{(m,-d)} = dim I_M^{(m+1, -d+2)}`,
i.e., `C(n,m) C(n,m+d) - f_m(M) f_{m+d}(M) = C(n,m+1) C(n,m+d-1) - f_{m+1}(M) f_{m+d-1}(M)`.

For uniforms: both sides 0 (trivial). For non-uniforms: this is a *specific condition* on the matroid's f-vector and the binomial coefficients.

## Why this didn't immediately give a proof

When restricted to the "complete bipartition" piece (= our X-restriction, which corresponds to Gui-Xiong's `H'_{n,n}` where `m = n`):

For the X-piece of `Λ ⊗ Λ*` (= `Λ^k ⊗ Λ*^{n-k}` for `k = 0, ..., n`): graded dimensions are `C(n, k)`, and Gui-Xiong's HL restricted here is **exactly the boolean Lefschetz** on the boolean lattice. That's just `L: x_A ⊗ y_{E \ A} \mapsto Σ x_{A ∪ {i}} ⊗ y_{(E \ A) \ {i}}` for `i ∈ E \ A` — i.e., the standard boolean simplicial coboundary.

For the matroid quotient at this X-piece: that's exactly our `∂*: X_k(M) → X_{k+1}(M)`.

So **Gui-Xiong on the X-piece reduces to boolean Lefschetz**, which we've been using all along. Their framework reformulates but doesn't actually crack the matroid restriction.

## The substantive open question (now clean)

The question becomes: **does the matroid ideal `I_M` restricted to the X-piece (= "X-restricted ideal") behave compatibly with L?**

For paving matroids: yes (proven via KW relaxation in PAPER.md).

For non-paving matroids: the X-restricted ideal contains more relations (= those induced by smaller circuits), and the L-image of these relations may or may not align with the ideal at the next bigrade.

This is the SAME GAP as before. Gui-Xiong's framework is a *cleaner formulation* of the gap, not a closure.

## What Gui-Xiong DOES give us

Despite not closing the proof, the connection is valuable:

1. **Confirms the framework is correct.** Our `L = Σ x_i ⊗ x_i` is exactly the Gui-Xiong operator. Our problem is "matroid quotient of Gui-Xiong".

2. **Free equivariance.** Gui-Xiong's HL is equivariant under any group. Our L on the matroid quotient inherits Aut(M)-equivariance automatically. (We knew this, but it's clean.)

3. **Hodge-Riemann positivity on the ambient.** Their HR gives positivity of specific bilinear forms on `Λ ⊗ Λ*`. The restriction to the matroid quotient inherits positivity (= positive semi-definite forms). This *might* be useful for further analysis but isn't immediately a proof.

4. **The matroid quotient as the obstruction.** The proof of ELC for non-paving matroids is the proof that the matroid ideal `I_M` interacts nicely with `L` at the X-restricted bigrades. This is a specific algebraic condition that could be testable.

## Concrete dimension-equality condition (testable)

For ELC of f-vector at bigrade `(m, d)` on matroid M to follow from Gui-Xiong's HL via the L-preservation argument:

> `(*)` :  `C(n, m) C(n, m+d) - f_m(M) f_{m+d}(M) = C(n, m+1) C(n, m+d-1) - f_{m+1}(M) f_{m+d-1}(M)`.

This is equivalent to:
`f_m f_{m+d} - f_{m+1} f_{m+d-1} = C(n, m) C(n, m+d) - C(n, m+1) C(n, m+d-1)`.

The right-hand side is a fixed function of `(n, m, d)` (a binomial-coefficient identity).

The left-hand side is the **matroid f-vector Newton-like difference** — closely related to f-vector log-concavity but specifically asking equality with the binomial benchmark.

For uniforms: both sides 0 (since `f_d = C(n, d)`).

For non-uniforms: both sides could be non-zero. Need to check.

**Test: is `(*)` true for paving matroids at our bigrades?** Let me suggest testing this empirically on our existing test matroids.

If `(*)` holds: we have a proof via Gui-Xiong.

If `(*)` fails: the proof requires more than Gui-Xiong's L-preservation; need to use the specific structure of `I_M`.

## Conclusion

Gui-Xiong's framework is the right *abstract setting* for our problem. Their L is our L. Their HL gives us a clean reformulation of what's needed.

But it doesn't immediately close the non-paving case. The condition `(*)` (= dimension equality on the matroid ideal) is the algebraic crux, and it depends specifically on the matroid's f-vector.

## A new clean proof of the paving case (via Gui-Xiong)

**Theorem (alternative proof of PAPER.md's main result, via Gui-Xiong).** *For every paving matroid M and every bigrade `(m, d)` with `2m + d ≤ n`, `m + d ≤ r`, `d ≥ 2`, the operator `L_quot: R(M)_m ⊗ R^∨(M)_{-(m+d)} → R(M)_{m+1} ⊗ R^∨(M)_{-(m+d-1)}` is injective.*

**Proof.** By Gui-Xiong (arXiv:2205.05420, Theorem 12), the ambient `L_amb = Σ x_i ⊗ x_i` on `Λ(V) ⊗ Λ(V*)` satisfies Hard Lefschetz; in particular `L_amb` restricted to bigrade `(m, -d)` is *injective* in the ascending direction (below middle).

For a paving matroid `M` of rank `r`: every subset of size `< r` is independent, so the matroid ideal `I_M ⊆ Λ(V)` is concentrated in degrees `≥ r`. The ideal at target bigrade `(m+1, -(d-2))` is
`I_M^target = I_M^{(m+1)} ⊗ Λ_{m+d-1}^* + Λ_{m+1} ⊗ I_M^{*(m+d-1)}`.
Both `m + 1 ≤ r - d + 1 ≤ r - 1 < r` and `m + d - 1 < r` (since `d ≥ 2`), so both `I_M^{(m+1)} = 0` and `I_M^{*(m+d-1)} = 0`, giving `I_M^target = 0`.

Then `(L_amb)^{-1}(I_M^target) = (L_amb)^{-1}(0) = ker(L_amb) = 0` (by Gui-Xiong HL injectivity at this bigrade). So `ker(L_quot) = π(0) / π(I_M^source) = 0`. ∎

**This is shorter than the KW relaxation proof.** It's also fully equivariant for free (= Gui-Xiong's HL is `G`-equivariant for any `G` acting on `V`).

## What we now understand about the gap

For non-paving matroids, the situation is structurally different:

- The matroid ideal has nontrivial contributions at degrees `< r` (= because of smaller circuits).
- At our bigrade `(m, -d)`, both source AND target carry ideal contributions.
- The injectivity of `L_quot` depends on the **specific algebraic interaction** between L's image and the ideal at the target.

The Gui-Xiong framework tells us *exactly* what we need:

> **Conjecture (Matroid Ideal Containment).** For every loopless matroid `M` and every bigrade `(m, d)` in our hypothesis, `(L_amb)^{-1}(I_M^target) ⊆ I_M^source` as subspaces of `(Λ ⊗ Λ*)_{(m, -d)}`.

This is a clean algebraic statement, no longer about operator rank but about *ideal preservation under L's preimage*.

## Why the conjecture should hold (heuristic)

Empirically across 200,000+ orbits: yes. The structural reason: when adding "smaller circuits" to a matroid (= making it non-paving), the matroid ideal gains relations at both the source and target bigrades. The L-action is designed to "move" mass between bigrades; the new ideal contributions at the target must "come from" corresponding ideal contributions at the source, mediated by L.

For paving M: source ideal might be nonzero, target ideal is 0, so the containment is trivial (preimage of 0 ⊆ anything).

For non-paving M: both nonzero. The containment requires *matroid-specific positivity* / *circuit-structure compatibility*.
